# Purpose

This document defines the approved method for creating the language system of the Curvature world.

The language must be historically grounded, internally consistent and honest about uncertainty.

---

# Foundational Principle

Curvature will not pretend that a complete, directly usable language of the early Polans has survived.

The language system will be built through:

1. historical reconstruction,
2. explicit evidence and confidence,
3. consistent construction rules,
4. controlled invention where evidence is missing,
5. later dialect and language evolution inside the world.

Proto-Slavic is the primary historical foundation.

The probable speech environment of early medieval West Slavic communities, including the Polans, is an important reference point.

---

# Classification

Every linguistic form should eventually be classified as one of:

- historically reconstructed,
- historically plausible,
- Curvature-derived,
- intentionally fictional,
- uncertain.

A created word must never be presented as a historically attested fact.

---

# Historical Reconstruction Layer

The reconstruction layer should record:

- reconstructed form,
- meaning,
- source or provenance,
- period,
- geographic relevance,
- phonological notes,
- morphological notes,
- confidence level,
- competing reconstructions,
- uncertainty.

This layer is evidence-oriented.

---

# Curvature Construction Layer

Historical evidence will not provide every word or grammatical structure needed by a living fictional world.

Missing elements may be created only through explicit rules.

Construction rules should define:

- allowed sounds,
- sound combinations,
- stress assumptions,
- inflection patterns,
- derivational morphology,
- compounding,
- semantic extension,
- naming patterns,
- treatment of new concepts,
- treatment of uncertain historical forms.

Created material should remain compatible with the selected historical base unless a documented world-development process changes it.

---

# Phonology

Future documentation should define:

- phoneme inventory,
- likely historical contrasts,
- vowels,
- consonants,
- palatalisation,
- syllable structure,
- stress assumptions,
- sound changes,
- permitted simplifications for players and voice systems.

No final phonology is approved yet.

Research and reconstruction must precede final decisions.

---

# Grammar

The future grammar should cover:

- noun cases,
- number,
- gender,
- adjective agreement,
- pronouns,
- verb aspect,
- tense and mood,
- participles,
- word order,
- negation,
- questions,
- derivation,
- forms of address.

The first Chronicle slice requires only a small, consistent and documented subset.

---

# Lexicon

The initial lexicon should prioritise words needed by the first world slice:

- people,
- kinship,
- settlement,
- landscape,
- weather,
- food,
- tools,
- animals,
- social roles,
- belief,
- danger,
- movement,
- time,
- greetings,
- requests,
- warnings,
- names.

Each entry should eventually record form, pronunciation, meaning, category, origin, confidence, grammar, examples and related forms.

---

# Names

Personal names, place names, divine names and cultural terms must follow the same linguistic rules.

Names must not be created through vague Slavic-sounding imitation.

They should be traceable to reconstructed roots, documented compounds, controlled Curvature derivation or world history.

---

# Dialects and Evolution

Future world development may produce:

- regional dialects,
- profession vocabulary,
- ritual language,
- archaisms,
- borrowings,
- sound changes,
- daughter languages.

Language evolution must be based on explicit rules and world history.

---

# Character Voice

A character's voice may reflect:

- origin,
- age,
- status,
- knowledge,
- beliefs,
- temperament,
- relationships,
- emotion,
- dialect,
- register.

Voice style must remain separate from factual world knowledge.

---

# Player Language

The player may use a modern natural language.

A Player Interpretation Layer will map that input into candidate world actions and meanings.

The world language remains independent.

The system may present translated dialogue, original forms, partial comprehension, glosses and learned vocabulary.

---

# Chronicle Language

The Chronicle may use:

- operator-facing modern language,
- translated world narration,
- original reconstructed or Curvature forms,
- quotations,
- archaic forms,
- character-specific speech.

The distinction must remain explicit.

---

# Initial Milestone

LANG-001 should produce:

- a research and provenance format,
- confidence categories,
- initial phonological assumptions,
- initial construction rules,
- a small lexicon for one place and several characters,
- naming rules,
- examples of dialogue and Chronicle usage.

It should not attempt to create a complete language in one sprint.

---

# Non-Goals

The first language milestone does not require:

- a complete dictionary,
- a complete historical grammar,
- academic certainty where none exists,
- a full spoken-language course,
- final voice synthesis,
- all future dialects.
